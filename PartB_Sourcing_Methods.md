Part B: Question 1 — Sourcing Methods (Targeting Precision Auto & Aerospace)
To scale the "Federer" company pipeline across India beyond basic Google searches, we must leverage structured B2B channels, industrial registries, and regulatory data layers. These sources inherently filter for high Systems Maturity (C7) and technical Differentiation (C3).

1. Certified Industrial Manufacturing Registries (AS9100 & IATF 16949)
Why it works for this ICP: High-end precision engineering companies operating in automotive and aerospace chains must hold global quality standard certifications (AS9100 for aerospace, IATF 16949 for automotive components). These registries map directly to our target profile because maintaining these certifications requires strict operational processes, documentation, and asset infrastructure.

Sourcing Strategy: Access the certified company directories directly through international registrar databases like TUV SUD, DNV, BSI Group, or the global IAQG (International Aerospace Quality Group) OASIS database, filtering by manufacturing hubs (e.g., Pune, Chennai, Ahmedabad).

Limitations: These registries verify operational capabilities and plant footprints but lack financial parameters, meaning we must cross-reference them later to check if they fit our Rs. 50Cr–500Cr revenue threshold.

2. Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR) Directory
Why it works for this ICP: True "Federer" companies invest heavily in internal capabilities rather than outsourcing. The Indian government’s DSIR maintains a rolling directory of officially "Recognized In-House R&D Units." This is a goldmine for identifying science-driven or highly differentiated operator-led manufacturers.

Sourcing Strategy: Extract data from the official, periodic DSIR publication directory. Filter by mechanical engineering, metallurgy, automotive components, and electronics across key industrial states (Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat).

Limitations: The registry updates are slow and do not capture immediate growth signals (like active hiring or recent capacity expansions).

3. MCA Filing Aggregators with Advanced Search Parameters (Tofler / Zauba Corp)
Why it works for this ICP: This is the most reliable way to enforce eligibility gates (Revenue between Rs. 50Cr–500Cr and active Indian corporate standing) before spending deep research time on a company.

Sourcing Strategy: Run targeted queries inside corporate data aggregators using specific criteria:

NIC Codes: 25 (Fabricated metal products), 29 (Motor vehicles and parts), 30 (Other transport equipment/Aerospace).

Location: Industrial clusters (Pune, Coimbatore, Vadodara).

Financial Filters: Revenue range set strictly between Rs. 50Cr and Rs. 500Cr.

Limitations: Financial filings in public registries can lag by 6 to 12 months. Fast-growing defense/aerospace firms might have already surged past the Rs. 500Cr cap within the current year.

4. Exhibitor Directories from Specialized Defense & Manufacturing Expos
Why it works for this ICP: Growth-minded, risk-taking promoters expanding their market footprints regularly exhibit at premier trade shows to secure defense contracts, global offset partnerships, and joint ventures.

Sourcing Strategy: Scrape the official exhibitor catalogs from recent major domestic trade shows, such as Aero India, DefExpo India, and the Pune Machine Tool Expo. Filter out non-manufacturing services to locate niche component builders.

Limitations: Trade directories contain a wide spectrum of companies—ranging from massive conglomerates (e.g., Bharat Forge) down to micro-scale component traders—requiring a swift, initial manual gate check to isolate mid-market companies.
